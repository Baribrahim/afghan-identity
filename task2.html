Explore Afghan Identity in My Own Life

What "Afghan Identity" Means to Me

For me, Afghan identity is not about where you live. It is about the values, traditions, and connections that families pass down through generations. I see it when my mother makes qabuli palaw for special days, when my father welcomes guests with warmth and respect, and when my grandparents tell stories about their life in Afghanistan before they moved to Saudi Arabia.

It is also about the language. We mix Dari and Pashto words in our family conversations. These words have feelings and meanings that English or German cannot really express.

Cultural Elements Present Daily

  • Family meals together, especially dinner
  • Speaking Dari/Pashto at home
  • Respectful greetings and formal address to elders
  • Hospitality shown to guests

Elements That Feel Weaker

  • Connection to extended family in Afghanistan
  • Understanding of regional traditions and customs
  • Participation in larger Afghan community events
  • Knowledge of Afghan history and geography
  • Traditional clothing and its cultural significance

How My Identity Has Changed

When I was a child in Germany, I had an Afghan identity but I did not really understand it. When I moved to Qatar, I met other Afghan families in the international community. This made me more aware of my cultural background. Now that I am getting older, I am trying hard to understand and strengthen this identity instead of just accepting it without thinking.

This project shows a change in me. Before, I took my culture for granted. Now I am actively learning about it, asking questions, and deciding what parts I want to keep for my future.
