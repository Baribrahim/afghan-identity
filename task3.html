Research Family as the Carrier of Culture

Conversations with My Parents

I spoke with my mother about how culture was passed to her. She explained that growing up in Saudi Arabia, her parents (my grandparents) made a conscious effort to maintain Afghan traditions. They would gather with other Afghan families in the community, celebrate Afghan holidays, and always speak Dari at home.

My father shared similar experiences, noting that even though they were far from Afghanistan, his parents ensured he understood his roots through stories, food, and language. They would tell him about life in Afghanistan—the mountains, the cities, the traditions—creating a connection to a place he had never visited.

Traditions My Family Still Practices

Nowruz Celebration

We celebrate the Persian New Year with a traditional haft-sin table, wearing new clothes, and visiting family. This tradition connects us to both Afghan and broader Persian cultural heritage.

Eid Celebrations

Both Eid al-Fitr and Eid al-Adha are celebrated with special prayers, family gatherings, traditional foods, and giving to those in need—maintaining the religious and cultural aspects of these holidays.

Family Storytelling

Regular family gatherings include stories from my grandparents about Afghanistan, creating an oral history that preserves memories and cultural knowledge.

Traditional Cooking

My mother and grandmother regularly prepare traditional Afghan dishes, teaching me recipes and the cultural significance behind certain foods and when they're served.

Specific Examples from My Household

Example 1: Every Friday, we have a family dinner where traditional Afghan dishes are served. This isn't just about food—it's a time for family connection, sharing stories, and maintaining our cultural practices.

Example 2: When guests visit, my parents always offer tea and sweets first, following the Afghan tradition of mehman nawazi (hospitality). This has been so ingrained that I do it automatically, even with non-Afghan friends.

Example 3: My grandmother has a collection of Afghan poetry books in Dari. She reads from them occasionally, and these poems carry cultural values, historical references, and linguistic beauty that connect us to Afghan literary tradition.
